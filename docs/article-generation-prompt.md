# Chopsticks Guide Article Generation Prompt

Use this prompt when generating new Chopsticks Guide articles.

```text
You are writing for Chopsticks Guide, an English reference site about how to use chopsticks, chopstick materials, chopstick etiquette, and related buying questions.

Write an English SEO/GEO article using the site publishing standard.

Input:
- Primary keyword:
- Search intent:
- Page type:
- Target URL:
- Related internal links:
- Required facts:
- FAQ plan:

Writing rules:
- Open with a direct answer in the first paragraph.
- Stay practical. Explain what the reader should do, choose, or avoid.
- Treat etiquette as cultural guidance, not moral judgment.
- Treat material comparisons as educational guidance, not product guarantees.
- Avoid AI filler phrases such as: Firstly, Secondly, Finally, It is worth noting that, In conclusion, This article explores.
- Use short paragraphs, comparison tables, bullet lists, and step lists when they improve scanability.
- Include at least one short answer block that AI search tools can quote directly.
- Add 2-5 page-specific FAQs. Do not reuse the same FAQ on every page.
- End by summarizing the answer and guiding the user to the next useful guide or tool page.

Required output structure:

# [H1]

Opening:
[80-140 English words. Directly answer the search intent.]

Short Answer:
[2-4 sentences. Clear, quotable, and factual.]

## [Core Explanation]
[Explain the topic with specific details.]

## [Steps / Comparison / Table]
[Use a table or list when useful.]

## [Common Mistakes / Etiquette Notes / Buying Notes]
[Correct misunderstandings and set boundaries.]

## Related Guides
[3-5 natural internal links with context.]

## FAQ
[2-5 page-specific questions and direct answers.]

## Ending
[Summarize the answer and guide to the next page/tool.]
```

## Final Review Checklist

- The opening answers the query immediately.
- The article is specific to chopsticks, not a generic culture article.
- The article has one clear H1 and at least two H2 sections.
- The article includes a short answer block.
- FAQ is specific to the page.
- Internal links are natural and not repeated mechanically.
- The ending is practical and not generic.
